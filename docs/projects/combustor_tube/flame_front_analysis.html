Distribution of flame front contours#

The flame front of the raw PIV images was detected using a self-made algorithm using Gaussian filters, enhancement of the images and eventually a binarization of the image. Fig. 3 shows an example.

flame_1_pdf_front_5000 and flame_2_pdf_front_5000 show the distribution of the flame front for 5000 images.

Average Flame angle#

The PDFs shown in flame_1_pdf_front_5000 and flame_2_pdf_front_5000 were used to find the average flame angle. As a first step, slices of the PDF were taken at different locations in axial direction, see flame_1_pdf_front_5000_slices and flame_2_pdf_front_5000_slices. The PDF data was smoothened using a Savitzky–Golay filter with a window length of 101 pixels and a polynomial order to of 3. It was chosen that the peaks of the filtered PDF data seen in the figures correspond to the average location of the flame front in the radial direction.

Finally, the average flame angle could be determined using a linear fit trhough the extracted average flame front locations, see flame_1_pdf_front_5000_angle and flame_2_pdf_front_5000_angle. The angles are 33.1\(^\circ\) and 41.4\(^\circ\), respectively.
